#### Abstract

Electromotive force measurements of the following cells: | $\begin{array}{l}\mathrm{Na} \text { ion-selective } \\ \text { electrode(glass) }\end{array}$ | $\begin{array}{c}\mathrm{NaBr}(\mathrm{m}), ~ \mathrm{MeOH}(\mathrm{w}), ~ \mathrm{H}_{2} \mathrm{O}(100-\mathrm{w}) \\ \text { or } \mathrm{EtOH}(\mathrm{w})\end{array}$ | $\begin{array}{l}\mathrm{Br} \text { ion-selective } \\ \text { electrode(solid state) }\end{array}$ |
| :--- | :---: | :--- | are carried out. The molal concentration m of NaBr in methanol-water mixed solvents changes from 0.01 m to 3.0 m and those in ethanol-water mixed solvents from 0.01 m to 5.0 m . The weight percent of methanol or ethanol in the mixed solvent w equals $0,10,20,40,60,80,90$ and 100 respectively. Calculations of standard cell potential of each conditions are made by using the extended Debye-Huckel equation and the Pitzer equation. The calculation results of standard potentials of both of the two equations are in good agreement with each other. The ionic association effect of NaBr in the mixed solvent are investigated by the Pitzer-Simonson equation extended to ion pairing.


## INTRODUCTION

Although electromotive force (EMF) measurements for $\mathrm{HX}(\mathrm{X}=\mathrm{Cl}, \mathrm{Br})$ in mixed solvents have considerably been made in dilute solutions, there are relatively few for salts in mixed solvents, and the salt concentrations investigated are also limited to dilute solutions. NaCl with the highest molal concentration $\mathrm{m}=2.0$ (Esteso et al., 1989), NaCl and KCl with the highest molal concentration $\mathrm{m}=1.0$ and 1.0 in the ethanol-water mixtures (Yang et al., 1979) have been investigated. In this paper the EMF measurements of the following cells are carried out.

| Na ion-selective | $\mathrm{NaBr}(\mathrm{m}), \mathrm{MeOH}(\mathrm{w}), \mathrm{H}_{2} \mathrm{O}(100-\mathrm{w})$ | Br ion-selective |
| :--- | :---: | :--- |
| electrode(glass) | or $\mathrm{EtOH}(\mathrm{w})$ | electrode(solid state) |

For the cells (1) using ion-selective electrodes, the Nernst equation is

$$
\begin{equation*}
\mathrm{E}=\mathrm{E}^{\circ}+2 \mathrm{k} \log \left(\mathrm{m}_{ \pm}\right) \tag{2}
\end{equation*}
$$

where $\mathrm{k}=2.303 \mathrm{RT} / \mathrm{F}$.
The $\mathrm{E}^{\circ}$ of the cells (1) in different solvent compositions are determined from three theoretical equations of electrolytic solutions and then the mean activity coefficients are calculated from Nernst equation (2) for every concentration studied. The ionic association effect of NaBr in methanol and in ethanol are investigated by the Pitzer-Simonson equation extended to ion pairing. According to this equation the association constant of NaBr in each case is evaluated from the experimental EMF data. Ion pairs are present for NaBr in the ethanol-water mixtures of high ethanol content as they have relatively low dielectric constant. Then the NaBr ionic association in the ethanol-water mixtures are also investigated.

## EXPERIMENTAL

## Chemicals

Methanol (or ethanol) of analytical grade is fractionally distilled using a column with about 30 theoretical plates. The intermediate fraction with constant top temperature is collected and constant refractive index is used as a purity measure.

Conductivity water is prepared from deionized water distilled in the presence of small amounts of $\mathrm{KMnO}_{4}$, and then distilled once more. Its specific conductivity is about $7 \times 10^{-7} \mathrm{~s}-\mathrm{cm}^{-1}$.

NaBr of analytical grade is recrystallized twice from the conductivity water. The purified solid NaBr salt is dried in an oven at $400^{\circ} \mathrm{C}$ for more than 10 hours. Then it is stored over silica gel in a dessicator before use.

## Instruments and Methods of Measurements

The electrodes used are a Na ion-selective electrode (glass, Orion Cat. No. 971100) and a Br ionselective electrode (solid state, Orion Cat. No. 943500). They are exactly treated before use and stored as stated in the instruction manual of the electrodes.

The cell used is a double-walled glass vessel. The NaBr stock solution of the proper solvent composition is added into the cell. The electrodes are immersed in the solution and the cell is sealed with Parafilm. The solution is stirred by a magnetic stirrer at a constant rate and is maintained at constant temperature of $298.15 \pm 0.005 \mathrm{~K}$ by circulation of thermostated water between the double wall of the cell. The thermostated water come from an ultra thermostat whose accuracy is about 0.002 K . The EMF is observed to be stable within $\pm 0.5 \mathrm{mV}$ for the period of about $1 / 2$ hours. The potential is measured by an Orion $811 \mathrm{PH} / \mathrm{mV}$ meter whose resolution is $\pm 0.1 \mathrm{mV}$. The meter is calibrated by means of Weston Standard Cell which has been calibrated by the Bureau of standard of Zhejiang Province of China.

The experimental EMF data by this method for each methanol-water (or ethanol-water) mixture as a function of the NaBr molal concentration m are shown in Table 1 and Table 2.

## DETERMINATION OF $\mathrm{E}^{\circ}$ BY THREE DIFFERENT EQUATIONS

## I). Extended Debye-Huckel Equation

For 1-1 electrolyte, the extended Debye-Huckel equation can be written as: (Harned et al. , 1958)

$$
\begin{equation*}
\log \gamma_{ \pm}=-A m^{1 / 2}\left(1+\operatorname{Bam}^{1 / 2}\right)+b m-\log (1+0.002 \mathrm{mM})+0.4343 \Gamma \tag{4}
\end{equation*}
$$

where $\mathrm{A}=1.824841 \times 10^{6}\left[\mathrm{~d} /(\mathrm{DT})^{3}\right]^{1 / 2}$

$$
\begin{equation*}
\mathrm{B}=50.29012[\mathrm{~d} /(\mathrm{DT})]^{1 / 2} \tag{5}
\end{equation*}
$$

$\Gamma$ is an extended term, for $1-1$ electrolyte, it can be expressed as: (Roy, R. A. et al. , 1973)

$$
\begin{equation*}
\Gamma=10^{-3} q^{3} f_{3}(X)+10^{-5} q^{5} f_{5}(X) \tag{7}
\end{equation*}
$$

where $q=N_{A} e^{2} /($ DRTa $) \quad X=\operatorname{Bam}^{1 / 2}$

$$
\begin{equation*}
\mathrm{f}_{3}(\mathrm{X})=0.06024-2.682 \mathrm{X}+4.935 \mathrm{X}^{2}-3.226 \mathrm{X}^{3}+0.5713 \mathrm{X}^{4}+0.1005 \mathrm{X}^{5} \tag{8}
\end{equation*}
$$

$$
\begin{equation*}
\mathrm{f}_{5}(\mathrm{X})=0.05674-3.555 \mathrm{X}+18.38 \mathrm{X}^{2}-32.99 \mathrm{X}^{3}+25.53 \mathrm{X}^{4}-7.303 \mathrm{X}^{5} \tag{9}
\end{equation*}
$$

## I )Pitzer Equation

For 1-1 electrolyte, the Pitzer equation (Pitzer 1973) can be written as

$$
\begin{equation*}
\ln \gamma_{ \pm}=f^{\gamma}+\mathrm{mB}^{\gamma}+\mathrm{m}^{2} \mathrm{C}^{\gamma} \tag{10}
\end{equation*}
$$

where

$$
\begin{equation*}
f^{\gamma}=-A_{\Phi}\left[m^{1 / 2} /\left(1+b m^{1 / 2}\right)+(2 / b) \ln \left(1+b m^{1 / 2}\right)\right] \tag{11}
\end{equation*}
$$

$$
\begin{equation*}
\mathrm{B}^{\gamma}=2 \beta^{(0)}+2 \beta^{(1)}\left[1-\left(1+\alpha \mathrm{m}^{1 / 2}-\alpha^{2} \mathrm{~m} / 2\right) \exp \left(-\alpha \mathrm{m}^{1 / 2}\right) /\left(\alpha^{2} \mathrm{~m}\right)\right. \tag{12}
\end{equation*}
$$

$$
\begin{equation*}
C^{\gamma}=(3 / 2) C^{\star} \tag{13}
\end{equation*}
$$

$$
\begin{equation*}
A_{\varphi}=(1 / 3)\left(2 \pi N_{A} d\right)^{1 / 2}\left[e^{2} /\left(4 \pi \varepsilon_{0} D k_{b} T\right)\right]^{3 / 2} \tag{14}
\end{equation*}
$$

After introducing all the fundamental physical constants into eq. (14), then it can be written as

$$
\begin{equation*}
\mathrm{A}_{\varphi}=1.4006 \times 10^{6} \mathrm{~d}^{1 / 2} /(\mathrm{DT})^{3 / 2} \tag{15}
\end{equation*}
$$

## II) Pitzer-Simonson Equation

The activity coefficient equation for NaBr in the mixed solvents can be derived from the $\mathrm{g}^{\mathrm{E}} / \mathrm{RT}$ expression presented by Pitzer and Simonson 1986.

$$
\begin{align*}
\mathrm{g}^{\mathrm{e}} / \mathrm{RT}= & \mathrm{x}_{1} \mathrm{x}_{2}\left[\mathrm{w}_{12}+\mathrm{u}_{12}\left(\mathrm{x}_{1}-\mathrm{x}_{2}\right)+\mathrm{Z}_{12} \mathrm{x}_{6}\right]+\mathrm{x}_{1} \mathrm{x}_{\mathrm{s}}\left[\mathrm{w}_{1 \mathrm{~s}}+2 / 3\left(1-\mathrm{x}_{1}+\mathrm{x}_{\mathrm{s}} / 2\right) \mathrm{u}_{1 \mathrm{~s}}\right] \\
& +\mathrm{x}_{2} \mathrm{x}_{\mathrm{s}}\left[\mathrm{w}_{2 \mathrm{~s}}+2 / 3\left(1-\mathrm{x}_{2}+\mathrm{x}_{\mathrm{s}} / 2\right) \mathrm{u}_{2 \mathrm{~s}}\right]-4 \mathrm{~A}_{\mathrm{x}} \mathrm{I}_{\mathrm{x}} \ln \left(1+\rho \mathrm{I}_{\mathrm{x}}^{1 / 2}\right) / \rho  \tag{16}\\
\ln \gamma_{ \pm(\mathrm{x})}= & -\mathrm{x}_{1} \mathrm{x}_{2}\left[\mathrm{w}_{12}+2\left(\mathrm{x}_{1}-\mathrm{x}_{2}\right) \mathrm{u}_{12}-\left(1-2 \mathrm{x}_{\mathrm{s}}\right) \mathrm{Z}_{12 \mathrm{a}}\right] \\
& +\mathrm{x}_{1}\left(1-\mathrm{x}_{\mathrm{a}}\right)\left[\mathrm{w}_{1 \mathrm{~s}}+2 / 3\left(1-\mathrm{x}_{1}+\mathrm{x}_{\mathrm{s}} / 2\right) \mathrm{u}_{1 \mathrm{~s}}\right]+\mathrm{x}_{1} \mathrm{x}_{\mathrm{s}}\left(3 \mathrm{x}_{1}+\mathrm{x}_{2}\right) \mathrm{u}_{1 \mathrm{~s}} / 3 \\
& +\mathrm{x}_{2}\left(1-\mathrm{x}_{\mathrm{s}}\right)\left[\mathrm{w}_{2 \mathrm{~s}}+2 / 3\left(1-\mathrm{x}_{2}+\mathrm{x}_{\mathrm{s}} / 2\right) \mathrm{u}_{2 \mathrm{~s}}\right]+\mathrm{x}_{2} \mathrm{x}_{\mathrm{s}}\left(3 \mathrm{x}_{2}+\mathrm{x}_{1}\right) \mathrm{u}_{2 \mathrm{~s}} / 3-\ln \gamma_{ \pm}^{*}(\mathrm{x}) \\
& -\mathrm{A}_{\mathrm{x}}\left[(2 / \rho) \ln \left(1+\rho I_{\mathrm{x}}^{1 / 2}\right)+1_{\mathrm{x}}^{1 / 2}\left(1-2 \mathrm{I}_{\mathrm{x}}\right) /\left(1+\rho 1_{\mathrm{x}}^{1 / 2}\right)\right] \tag{17}
\end{align*}
$$

where $\gamma_{\dot{1}}\left(x_{x}^{\circ}\right)$ is the infinite dilusion activity coefficient. $\mathrm{A}_{x}$ is the Debye-Huckel parameter and

$$
\begin{equation*}
A_{x}=(1 / 3)\left(2 \pi N_{A} d / M\right)^{1 / 2}\left[e^{2} /\left(D k_{b} T\right)\right]^{3 / 2}=4.439 \times 10^{7}\left[(d / M) /(D T)^{3}\right]^{1 / 2} \tag{18}
\end{equation*}
$$

$\rho=2150[\mathrm{~d} /(\mathrm{DT})]^{1 / 2}$ which is related to a hard core collision diameter (Pitzer, 1980). The activity coefficient of the electrolyte in the mole fraction basis $\gamma_{ \pm(x)}$ can be converted into the molality basis $\gamma_{ \pm}$. The relationship between them is

$$
\begin{equation*}
\log Y_{ \pm}=\log Y_{ \pm(x)}-\log [1+0.002 \mathrm{mM}] \tag{19}
\end{equation*}
$$

The ionic association effect of NaBr in the solvent are investigated by the Pitzer-Simonson equation extended to ion pairing. Take NaBr -methanol binary system as an example. Let the fraction of NaBr associated is $\theta$. Then the mole fraction of free ion $\mathrm{x}_{1}\left(\mathrm{i}=\mathrm{Na}^{+}\right.$, or $\left.\mathrm{Br}^{-}\right)$, of ion pair $\mathrm{x}_{\mathrm{p}}$, and of solvent methanol $\mathbf{x}_{1}$ can be expressed as.

$$
\begin{equation*}
\mathbf{x}_{1}=(1-\theta) \mathbf{n}_{2} /\left[\mathbf{n}_{1}+(2-\theta) \mathbf{n}_{3}\right] \tag{20}
\end{equation*}
$$

$$
\begin{equation*}
\mathrm{x}_{\mathrm{p}}=\theta \mathrm{n}_{\mathrm{i}} /\left[\mathrm{n}_{1}+(2-\theta) \mathrm{n}_{3}\right] \tag{21}
\end{equation*}
$$

$$
\begin{equation*}
\mathrm{x}_{1}=\mathrm{n}_{1} /\left[\mathrm{n}_{1}+(2-\theta) \mathrm{n}_{8}\right] \tag{22}
\end{equation*}
$$

$$
\begin{equation*}
\mathrm{I}_{\mathrm{z}}=1 / 2\left(\mathrm{x}_{1} \mathrm{Z}_{k a}^{2}++\mathrm{x}_{1} \mathrm{Z}_{k}^{2}-\right)=\mathrm{x}_{1} \tag{23}
\end{equation*}
$$

The expression of equilibrium constant of ion-pairing can be obtained from the derivative of Gibbs free energy of mixing with respect to $\theta$ equated to zero. The Gibbs free energy of mixing can be
expressed by the Pitzer-Simonson Equation extended to ion pairing. (Pitzer and Simonson, 1984)

$$
\begin{align*}
\frac{\triangle_{m} G}{R T}= & n_{1} \ln x_{1}+\theta n_{1} \ln x_{p}+2(1-\theta) n_{1} \ln x_{1}+n_{1} x_{1}\left(w_{1 s}+x_{1} u_{1}\right) \\
& -4 A_{x}(1-\theta) n_{2} \ln \left(1+\rho I_{x}^{1 / 2}\right) / \rho-\theta n_{a} \ln K_{x} \tag{24}
\end{align*}
$$

where $\mathrm{x}_{4}=1-\mathrm{x}_{1}$ is independent from $\theta$
$\frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d} \theta}\left(\frac{\triangle_{\mathrm{m}} \mathrm{G}}{\mathrm{RT}}\right)=\mathrm{n}_{\mathrm{z}} \ln \mathrm{x}_{\mathrm{p}}-2 \mathrm{n}_{\mathrm{b}} \ln \mathrm{x}_{1}-\mathrm{n}_{\mathrm{z}} \ln K_{\mathrm{x}}+2 \mathrm{~A}_{\mathrm{x}} \mathrm{n}_{\mathrm{z}}\left[(2 / \rho) \ln \left(1+\rho \mathrm{I}_{\mathrm{x}}{ }^{1 / 2}\right)+\mathrm{I}_{\mathrm{x}}{ }^{1 / 2}\left(1-\mathrm{I}_{\mathrm{x}}\right) /\left(1+\rho \mathrm{I}_{\mathrm{x}}{ }^{1 / 2}\right)\right]$
Put $\frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d} \theta}\left(\frac{\triangle_{\mathrm{m}} \mathrm{G}}{\mathrm{RT}}\right)=0$, then the following equation can be obtained.

$$
\begin{equation*}
\mathrm{K}_{\mathrm{x}}=\left(\mathrm{x}_{\mathrm{p}} / \mathrm{x}_{\mathrm{i}}^{2}\right) \exp \left\{2 \mathrm{~A}_{\mathrm{x}}\left[(2 / \rho) \ln \left(1+\rho \mathrm{I}_{\mathrm{x}}^{1 / 2}\right)+\mathrm{I}_{\mathrm{x}}^{1 / 2}\left(1-\mathrm{I}_{\mathrm{x}}\right) /\left(1+\rho \mathrm{I}_{\mathrm{x}}^{1 / 2}\right)\right]\right\} \tag{25}
\end{equation*}
$$

For the system including ion pair, eq(2) and eq(19) are revised to

$$
\begin{equation*}
\mathrm{E}=\mathrm{E}^{\circ}+2 \mathrm{k} \log \left[(1-\theta) \mathrm{m} \gamma_{ \pm}\right] \tag{26}
\end{equation*}
$$

$$
\begin{equation*}
\log _{ \pm}=\log Y_{ \pm(x)}-\log [1+0.001(2-\theta) \mathrm{mM}] \tag{27}
\end{equation*}
$$

For a given $\mathrm{K}_{\mathrm{x}}$, the $\theta$ value at every molality can be obtained by eq. (25), and then from eq. (17) (put $\mathrm{x}_{2}=0$ reduced to binary system) eq. (26), eq. (27), $\mathrm{E}^{\circ}, \mathrm{w}_{1 \mathrm{~s}}, \mathrm{u}_{1 \mathrm{~s}}$, can be optimized from the experimental EMF data and the root mean square deviation is calculated. If at some definite value of $\mathbf{K}_{\mathbf{x}}$, the deviation is mininum, it means that this $\mathrm{K}_{\mathrm{x}}$ can fit the experimental data best, then this value of $\mathrm{K}_{\mathrm{x}}$ can be taken as association constant. The simplex method is chosen to optimize $\mathrm{K}_{x}, \mathrm{E}^{\circ}, \mathrm{w}_{18}, \mathrm{u}_{1,}$ by using such calculation procedure. The results of $\mathrm{K}_{\mathrm{x}}, \mathrm{E}^{\circ}$ are shown in Table 4. The association constant of NaBr in pure methanol is $\mathrm{K}_{\mathrm{x}}=24.77$ in mole fraction basis, or $\mathrm{K}_{\mathrm{m}}=0.79$ in molality basis. The $\theta$ values at every experimental molality can be calculated from $\mathrm{K}_{\mathrm{x}}$ value, and it is only $5 \%$ at the highest concentration measured. So NaBr is only weakly associated in methanol, and there is nearly no ionic association in methanol-water mixed solvents due to their dielectric constants being much larger than pure methanol. So that in the calculation of NaBr -methanol-water ternary system, the effect of ionic association is not considered. By combining eq. (17), eq. (19) and eq. (2), $\mathrm{E}^{\circ}$ and $\mathrm{Z}_{123}$ are optimized from experimental EMF data, where the binary parameters $w_{12}, u_{12}, w_{12}, u_{12}, w_{23}, u_{23}$ are calculated from corresponding binary systems.

For NaBr -ethanol binary system, the calculation result is $\mathrm{K}_{\mathrm{x}}=946.81$. This shows that NaBr is strongly associated in pure ethanol. So the association of NaBr in ethanol-water mixed solvent must be considered. In the calculation of NaBr -ethanol-water ternary system, $\mathrm{w}_{1,} \mathrm{u}_{1,}$ can not be used as constant to fit the experimental EMF data of the ternary system. First due to the association of NaBr must be considered. Second, from the experimental EMF data of NaBr - ethanol binary system, see Table 2, $w_{1,}$ and $u_{1,}$ can be optimized only in the range of very low NaBr concentration. So they can not be used as constant. Let $w_{12}$ and $u_{18}$ depend on the solvent composition under the condition $z_{122}=0$. In the calculation of $\mathrm{K}_{\mathrm{z}}$ for NaBr -ethanol-water ternary system, eq. (25) still can be used except $x_{1}, x_{p}, I_{x}$ are defined as.

$$
\begin{equation*}
\mathrm{x}_{1}=(1-\theta) \mathrm{n}_{2} /\left[\mathrm{n}_{1}+\mathrm{n}_{2}+(2-\theta) \mathrm{n}_{3}\right] \tag{28}
\end{equation*}
$$

$$
\begin{equation*}
\mathrm{x}_{\mathrm{p}}=\theta \mathrm{n}_{2} /\left[\mathrm{n}_{1}+\mathrm{n}_{2}+(2-\theta) \mathrm{n}_{4}\right] \tag{29}
\end{equation*}
$$

$$
\begin{equation*}
\mathrm{I}_{\mathrm{x}}=\mathrm{X}_{1} \tag{30}
\end{equation*}
$$

## CONCLUSION

The experimental EMF data of aqueous NaBr solutions for the different NaBr molal concentrations combining with the calculated $\log \gamma_{ \pm}$values show that the Nernst equation is obeyed very well. This verifies the reliability of the experimental work.

The standard patentials $\mathrm{E}^{\circ}$ of NaBr in different compositions of mixed solvent are determined by three different equation , Extended Debye-Huckel, Pitzer , and Pitzer Simonson equation. The $\mathrm{E}^{\circ}$ values obtained are in good agreement with each other. As the Pitzer equation can correlate all the experimental EMF data with rather small deviations, the mean activity coefficients of NaBr under different conditions in Table 1 and Table 2 are calculated from Pitzer equation.

The ionic association effect of NaBr in the solvent are investigated by the Pitzer-Simonson equation extended to ion pairing. The results show that NaBr is only weakly associated in pure methanol with association constant $\mathrm{K}_{\mathrm{x}}=24.77$, but is strongly associated in pure ethanol with $\mathrm{K}_{\mathrm{x}}=$ 946. 81. The association constants of NaBr in the ethanol - water mixed solvents are also determined. The values of $\mathrm{K}_{1}$ in 80 and 90 weight percent of ethanol in ethanol-water mixed solvents are 159.41, 519.91 respectively. These values are calculated from the experimental EMF data rather than from the direct experiments. They are only the estimated values.
